Nature in the Suburban Gothic

The suburbs are defined by their physical location—a close proximity to the convenience of urban life combined with a contained form of nature. The manicured lawn, a classic suburban status symbol, embodies this spirit: well-grown, but not overgrown; doused with chemicals, yet still natural. But when this fine balance of controlled growth and natural spirit breaks down, the suburbs find themselves besieged and encroached on by nature. The following set of photographs attempts to describe the conflict between suburban life and one of its defining traits.
